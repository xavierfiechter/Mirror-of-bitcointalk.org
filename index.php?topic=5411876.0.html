UselessShit.co is a fun project which purpose is to spread the good vibes and encourage people into learning about bitcoin and about the importance of sats stacking. It was inspired by Der Gigi's https://fuckingshitcoins.com nearly two months ago. Since then I've been working on developing new features and getting more accustomed to React (I have over 6 years of commercial experience in Angular). As I'm gathering more and more feedback, the project is on it's way to become (contrary to what the domain name suggests 😅) a useful tool for spreading bitcoin awareness (at least I believe so). I'm also considering rebranding to https://thisbitcointhing.com in the future.

Features

  • business-sized cards/bookmarks generator - create personalized items, include lightning gifts & hand them to your friends & foes;
  • quickly calculate the amount of sats from fiat input & generate price receipts;
  • bitcoin resources - a list of people to follow in bitcoin space, books, podcasts and more;

Really like sites like these. Simple, homegrown sites without anything crazy.
My only criticism as a web developer for 15+ years, would be the following (and I mean this with the best intentions, btw - because I can tell how much effort you put into the site)

1. I would sway away from rotating slideshow on the homepage. Honestly, I think if you used the following image as a standard image (https://uselessshit.co/images/card-2.jpeg) it would be much less anxiety inducing on the user. Finding a way to place the other images (if you want them) on the page, might be advisable. Remember, you're bringing new users to Bitcoin (and these foreign concepts) to the site, having a slideshow showing technical jargon right away probably isn't the best way to keep your user calmly scrolling down the page)
2. I would reduce the amount of verbiage to get to the basics. TBH and in full transparency (and quite embarrassingly) I had no clue what stacking sats was, and linking to another site (under the "were you handed a card") is probably just bad flow. I actually ended up googling it to find out what it was, vs reading the site - which is probably a bad sign. I would perhaps take the most important points you're trying to make, make them concise, and include them plainly in the first 1-2 minute read of your site. You'll get more traction this way.

Otherwise, great job! Clean design & code looks good!
