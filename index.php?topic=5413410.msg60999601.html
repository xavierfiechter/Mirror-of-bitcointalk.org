You are paying people directly with Bitcoin, and that Bitcoin is held in custodial or non-custodial wallets?


 We have a cargo delivery service in the state of New York and the customer pays for the execution of an order for the delivery of a certain cargo directly to the courier through the cache or through a banking application.

Bitcoin in our service is used only by couriers to insure their delivery orders. The price of the order insurance is specified by the customer when creating an application for additional insurance. For example, in the case of ordering the delivery of a bouquet of flowers with insurance of $5, an amount of $5 will be deducted from the courier's account for the duration of the order, after completing this order, the courier will return it $5 and our commission for the completed order will be deducted 10% of the delivery amount

We do not create bitcoin wallets for couriers, we do not store them. Bitcoin is used as a way to deposit money into our system, to insure parcels. Now we are creating a FAQ for a better explanation of how the Bringa courier service works
